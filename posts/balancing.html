Balancing Pressure and Purpose: What My Mom Taught Me About Resilience

By Taahirah Denmark — October 24, 2025

The other day, my mom sent me a message that stopped me in my tracks. It wasn’t harsh—it was honest. She reminded me that life doesn’t wait, that bills keep coming, and that sometimes you have to take jobs you don’t love just to stay afloat. I knew she meant well, but part of me felt unseen.

I’ve been working toward a career in software engineering, building projects, applying to roles, and trying to build a life that’s different from what she knew growing up. For her, stability meant having a steady paycheck. For me, stability means having purpose—doing something I care about, even if it takes longer to get there.

“Sometimes love looks like pressure. Sometimes motivation sounds like criticism.”

I understand her perspective now more than ever. She’s from a generation that didn’t have the flexibility to dream about startups, AI projects, or building side businesses. Their path was linear: get a job, stay there, provide. Mine looks different. It’s nonlinear, experimental, sometimes messy—but it’s mine.

So instead of arguing, I decided to use her words as fuel. To keep coding. To keep learning. To prove—not through defiance—but through consistency. Every project I build, every skill I master, is a quiet way of saying, “I’m working on it.”

I’ve realized that my mom’s expectations aren’t my enemy—they’re a reflection of her love, her fears, and her hope that I’ll be okay. And in a way, she’s right. Something is better than nothing. But the real balance is in doing something that brings stability and meaning.

Growing up, I used to think success was about proving people wrong. Now I know it’s about proving yourself right—by staying true to your path, even when others don’t fully understand it.

Thanks for reading — if you’ve ever felt the same kind of pressure from someone you love, you’re not alone.
